AI Token Metrix · Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 2026-07-14 (Version 1.0)
Last Updated: 2026-07-14

AI Token Metrix ("the App", "we", "our") is a macOS menu-bar utility that parses local log files produced by AI coding tools on your device to compute token usage and estimated cost. This Privacy Policy explains what information the App processes, why, and your rights.

The App is designed around a single principle: your usage data never leaves your device.

1. Data Controller

Controller: W PF, operating as an independent developer.
Contact: tokenusagemonitor@gmail.com

This identifies:

  • The "data controller" under the EU / UK GDPR and the "business" under the California CCPA / CPRA.
  • The "personal information handler" (个人信息处理者) under China's PIPL.

2. Information the App Processes

The App processes only the following categories of information, and only on your device. Nothing in this section is transmitted to us or to any third party.

2.1 AI Tool Log Files (Read-Only, User-Authorized)

When you explicitly authorize the App to access a directory using the macOS system file picker (NSOpenPanel), the App reads log files created by AI coding tools you have installed. Depending on which providers you enable, the paths include:

ProviderPath
Claude Code~/.claude/projects/*.jsonl
OpenAI Codex CLI~/.codex/sessions/**/rollout-*.jsonl, ~/.codex/archived_sessions/
Kimi CLI~/.kimi/sessions/**/wire.jsonl, plus ~/.kimi/config.json to read the current model name
Kimi Code~/.kimi-code/sessions/**/wire.jsonl
Gemini CLI~/.gemini/tmp/**/chats/*.{json,jsonl}
Google Antigravity~/.gemini/antigravity/.token-monitor/rpc-cache/v1/*.jsonl
OpenCode~/.local/share/opencode/storage/message/**/*.json
GitHub Copilot CLI~/.copilot/otel/*.jsonl (only if you have set the environment variable COPILOT_OTEL_ENABLED=true)

From each log entry, the App extracts:

  • Token counts (input / output / cached / reasoning)
  • Model name (e.g., claude-sonnet-4-5)
  • Timestamp
  • Session ID — generated by the AI tool, not by us
  • Working directory (cwd) — the path where you invoked the AI tool

About cwd: the working directory path may contain your project name (e.g., /Users/you/Projects/company-secret-project). We treat this as personal information under GDPR and PIPL. It is stored only locally, used only for grouping your usage by project inside the App, and is never transmitted.

The App does not read the contents of your conversations with the AI tools. Only the usage metadata described above is extracted.

2.2 App Configuration

Stored locally on your device:

  • Directory authorization data (macOS security-scoped bookmark)
  • Which providers you have enabled or paused
  • Display currency preference, week-start preference, language preference
  • Budget rules you configured (limits, alert thresholds, snooze state)
  • Menu-bar popover behavior preferences

2.3 Aggregated Usage Statistics

The App computes derived data from the raw log entries above:

  • Daily totals per (provider × model × session) bucket
  • Daily summary — one record per day, covering all providers combined

These aggregates are stored in a local SwiftData database inside the App's sandbox and App Group container.

2.4 Network Requests (Outbound-Only, No Personal Data Sent)

To display cost in your preferred currency and to keep model pricing up to date, the App makes outbound HTTPS requests approximately every 12 hours to the following public data sources:

  • https://models.dev/api.json — pricing data for ~2,900 AI models
  • https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@fawazahmed0/currency-api/... — foreign-exchange rates
  • https://api.frankfurter.dev/... — fallback FX rates (European Central Bank data)
  • https://raw.githubusercontent.com/BerriAI/litellm/... served via jsDelivr / jsdmirror — fallback pricing data

These requests contain no personal information. They only download public reference data. Your usage counts, project names, session IDs, or any other information about you are never uploaded.

The App does not use analytics SDKs, telemetry SDKs, crash-reporting services, advertising SDKs, or any third-party tracking library.

2.5 Local Notifications

If you configure a Budget Rule, the App may deliver local notifications via the macOS UserNotifications framework. These notifications are generated entirely on your device and are not routed through Apple Push Notification service (APNs). Notification content includes the provider ID (e.g., claude) and a percentage-of-budget indicator.

The App requests notification permission only the first time you save a Budget Rule — not at launch — so it does not ask for permission it does not need.

3. How the App Uses This Information

  • Compute and display your AI usage and estimated cost in the menu-bar interface, the detail window, and the Widget.
  • Aggregate usage into daily summaries for trend visualizations.
  • Deliver local notifications when your usage crosses a Budget Rule threshold you configured.
  • Persist your preferences across App launches.

The App does not use your information for advertising, profiling, sale, cross-context behavioral advertising, or automated decision-making.

4. Where Data Is Stored

All information is stored exclusively on your device, in the following locations under your macOS user account:

  • Sandbox container: ~/Library/Containers/com.wpf.aitokenmetrix/Data/
  • App Group container (shared with the Widget extension): ~/Library/Group Containers/group.com.wpf.aitokenmetrix/

These directories are protected by the macOS sandbox and are not accessible to other apps.

System iCloud Backup: If your device's system settings enable iCloud Backup for App data, portions of this data may be included in Apple's iCloud Backup of your device. In that case Apple acts as data processor and is subject to Apple's own Privacy Policy — not this one.

Future feature — iCloud sync: A future version of the App may synchronize the "daily summary" table across your own Apple devices via iCloud (CloudKit private database). If this feature is added, the synchronized data will remain in your own iCloud account; Apple would process this data on your behalf, and it would not be accessible to us. This feature is not yet enabled. When it launches, this Privacy Policy will be updated, and you will be notified in-App before the change takes effect.

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, share, or transfer your personal information to any third party.

The only third-party interactions are the outbound HTTPS requests listed in §2.4, which download public reference data (pricing tables, exchange rates). These requests do not carry any information about you or your usage.

The App does not integrate with any analytics, advertising, social-login, or crash-reporting service.

6. Data Retention

  • Local data is retained on your device indefinitely, until you delete it.
  • You can stop data collection at any time by disabling the relevant provider in the App's Settings, or by revoking directory authorization.

6.1 How to Delete Your Data

Because all data is local, you can delete it in any of the following ways:

  1. Uninstall the App: drag it to the Trash. This removes the App binary but may leave data in ~/Library/Containers/ and ~/Library/Group Containers/.
  2. Delete data directories manually: remove the directories listed in §4.

Known limitation: The current version of the App does not yet provide an in-App "Delete all data" button. This is a planned improvement (see §7, right to erasure). Until it ships, please use one of the methods above.

8. Children's Privacy

The App is intended for adult developers working with AI coding tools. We do not knowingly process information from children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). If you believe a child has used the App, please contact us; because all data is local to the child's device, deletion is under the child's / guardian's direct control.

9. Security

  • All data is stored inside the macOS sandbox and App Group container, protected by macOS's user-level access controls.
  • All outbound network requests use HTTPS.
  • No credentials or authentication tokens are collected by the App at this time. (A future feature may support official cloud APIs; if that ships, credentials will be stored in the macOS Keychain — never in plaintext.)
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs are embedded.

10. International Data Transfers

Because all personal data is processed on your device and not transmitted to us or any third party, there are no international data transfers under this Privacy Policy.

The outbound requests in §2.4 fetch public reference data from CDNs that may be hosted in various regions; because those requests carry no personal information, they do not constitute an international transfer of personal data under GDPR or PIPL.

Future iCloud sync feature: if enabled by you in a future version, synchronization occurs via your own iCloud account. The region where Apple stores your iCloud data is determined by your Apple ID account settings and Apple's own Privacy Policy — not by us.
